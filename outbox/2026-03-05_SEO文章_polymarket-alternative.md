## Why Polymarket Isn't Enough Anymore

Polymarket deserves credit. It popularized on-chain prediction markets and demonstrated real product-market fit during the 2024 US election cycle, hitting over $3.5 billion in trading volume. But its structural limitations are real:

**Single-platform view.** Polymarket only shows its own markets. If Kalshi has sharper odds on a Fed rate decision, you won't know unless you manually check both platforms.

**Crypto-only payments.** USDC on Polygon is the entry point. There's no fiat gateway, which creates friction for traders coming from traditional finance backgrounds.

**No smart money layer.** Perhaps most critically: you can see aggregate market volume, but you cannot see *who* is moving it. Institutional players and high-conviction whales trade on Polymarket — and there is no native tool to follow them.

**Limited market types.** Polymarket focuses on binary yes/no outcomes. More nuanced markets — continuous probability, multi-outcome brackets — are either missing or underdeveloped.

The prediction market space has matured enough that specialized tools now exist to solve each of these gaps. Here are the five most effective alternatives.

### 2. Kalshi — Best for Regulated US Markets

Kalshi is a CFTC-regulated prediction market exchange operating in the United States. This matters enormously for institutional participants and risk-averse traders who need regulatory clarity. Kalshi offers both fiat deposits and crypto, covers economic indicators, political events, and weather outcomes, and has been expanding its market catalog aggressively since its federal regulatory win in late 2024.

The tradeoff: Kalshi's interface is cleaner but less data-rich than what power traders want. It also doesn't aggregate outside its own ecosystem.

**Best for:** US-based traders, fiat-first workflows, compliance-sensitive use cases.
