## What Is Polymarket?

Polymarket is a decentralized prediction market built on the Polygon blockchain. It launched in 2020 and has grown to become the world's largest prediction market by volume.

**How it works:**
Users deposit USDC (a stablecoin) and trade YES/NO shares on event outcomes. Prices are set by the market and reflect the collective probability estimate. When events resolve, YES shares pay $1, NO shares pay $0.

**Who it's for:**
Crypto-native users globally (excluding the US) who want access to a wide range of markets with high liquidity and low fees.

---

## What Is Kalshi?

Kalshi is a centralized prediction market exchange regulated by the CFTC (Commodity Futures Trading Commission). It launched in 2021 and became the first entity to receive CFTC approval to operate a prediction market for US users.

**How it works:**
Users deposit USD and trade event contracts. Kalshi operates as a regulated exchange with formal clearing and settlement processes—similar to how a futures exchange works.

**Who it's for:**
US-based users who want a legally compliant, fiat-friendly prediction market experience.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

### US Legal Access
**Winner: Kalshi**

This is the most important practical difference. Polymarket explicitly blocks US IP addresses due to regulatory concerns. US traders who try to use Polymarket violate its terms of service.

Kalshi, by contrast, is CFTC-regulated and fully legal for US users. If you're in the US and want real-money prediction markets, Kalshi is the only major compliant option.

---

### Volume and Liquidity
**Winner: Polymarket**

Polymarket consistently processes 5-10x more monthly volume than Kalshi. Higher volume means tighter spreads, less slippage on larger trades, and more active markets.

For large trades ($10,000+), Polymarket's liquidity advantage is significant. For smaller trades ($100-1,000), both platforms are generally liquid on major markets.

---

### Market Selection
**Winner: Polymarket**

Polymarket lists 1,000+ active markets across politics, crypto, sports, science, and entertainment. New markets launch frequently, often within hours of a news event.

Kalshi has 500+ markets but is more selective—it focuses on events with clear, verifiable outcomes and regulatory approval for each market category.

Polymarket charges 0% trading fees on standard markets. There are no explicit platform fees; you only pay the natural market spread set by liquidity providers.

Kalshi uses a formula-based fee structure: `0.07 × contracts × price × (1 - price)` for takers, which works out to up to ~1.75% on contracts priced near 50¢. S&P 500 and Nasdaq markets are half price (~0.875% max).

### Deposit and Withdrawal
**Winner: Kalshi** (for most users)

Kalshi accepts USD bank transfers and operates like a traditional financial account. Deposits and withdrawals work like any regulated brokerage.

Polymarket requires USDC (a cryptocurrency). New users need to first acquire crypto, transfer it to a wallet, and then deposit. This is a significant barrier for non-crypto users.

---

### Regulatory Protection
**Winner: Kalshi**

As a CFTC-regulated exchange, Kalshi has formal protections: client funds are segregated, there's a regulated dispute process, and the platform operates under federal oversight.

Polymarket is a decentralized protocol. There's no regulatory recourse if something goes wrong, and smart contract risks (though low) exist.

### Market Types and Categories
**Roughly equal**

Both platforms cover major political events (elections, legislation), economic indicators (Fed rates, GDP), and sports. Polymarket tends to list more speculative and crypto-specific markets. Kalshi tends toward more mainstream financial and economic events.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can you use Polymarket or Kalshi in the US?**
Kalshi is the only CFTC-regulated prediction market and is fully legal for US users. Polymarket blocks US IP addresses and is not accessible to US residents under its terms of service.

**Which prediction market has better odds—Polymarket or Kalshi?**
It depends on the market. Because they operate independently, the same event can price at different odds on each platform. Checking both (or using an aggregator like MoonX) before trading is the best way to find the optimal entry.

**Which came first, Kalshi or Polymarket?**
Polymarket launched in 2020, Kalshi in 2021. Polymarket grew faster initially due to its open, permissionless structure.

**Is Polymarket safe?**
Polymarket operates on smart contracts and has processed billions in volume without major hacks. The primary risks are smart contract bugs (low probability) and the regulatory risk that the platform could be forced to shut down for US users.

**Is Kalshi legit?**
Yes. Kalshi is CFTC-regulated, which means it meets the legal requirements to operate as a prediction market exchange in the United States. Client funds are held separately from company funds.
