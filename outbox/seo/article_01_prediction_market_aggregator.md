# What Is a Prediction Market Aggregator?

Prediction markets have exploded in popularity. Polymarket hit $500M+ in monthly volume. Kalshi became the first CFTC-regulated platform. Manifold built a community of thousands of forecasters. And dozens more platforms have emerged to meet demand.

There's just one problem: **they're all separate.**

If you want access to the best odds on any given event, you need accounts on multiple platforms, capital spread across different wallets, and time to check each one manually. Until recently, there was no way around it.

That's where a prediction market aggregator comes in.

---

## What Is a Prediction Market Aggregator?

A prediction market aggregator is a platform that pulls together markets, odds, and liquidity from multiple prediction market platforms into a single unified interface.

Instead of choosing between Polymarket or Kalshi, you can:

- **See all markets in one place** — compare the same event across platforms
- **Find the best odds** — spot when Polymarket prices an event at 65% and Kalshi at 60%
- **Trade from one account** — no need to manage multiple wallets or deposits
- **Discover markets you'd miss** — each platform lists different events; an aggregator shows them all

Think of it like Google Flights for prediction markets. Rather than checking each airline's website individually, you get all options in one search.

---

## How Does a Prediction Market Aggregator Work?

At its core, an aggregator does three things:

**1. Data aggregation**
The platform continuously fetches live market data from connected platforms—current YES/NO prices, total volume, liquidity depth, and resolution dates. This data is normalized so you can compare apples to apples across platforms with different pricing formats.

**2. Unified interface**
Instead of logging into Polymarket, then Kalshi, then Manifold separately, you see all active markets in one dashboard. You can filter by category (politics, crypto, sports), by platform, or by volume.

**3. Smart routing (advanced)**
Some aggregators can route your trade to whichever platform offers the best price at the moment you execute—similar to how DEX aggregators like 1inch route swaps for best execution.

## Why Does It Matter? The Odds Arbitrage Opportunity

Here's something most prediction market traders don't realize: **the same event often trades at different odds on different platforms simultaneously.**

For example, on a given day you might see:
- "Will the Fed cut rates in March?" — Polymarket: YES at 72%
- "Will the Fed cut rates in March?" — Kalshi: YES at 68%

That's a 4-point spread. If you're betting YES, you want Polymarket's 72¢. If you're betting NO, you want Kalshi's 32¢ (100 - 68).

Without an aggregator, you'd need to check both platforms manually and act fast before the prices converge. With an aggregator, you see this immediately.
