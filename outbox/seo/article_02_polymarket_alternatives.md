## Why People Look for Polymarket Alternatives

Before ranking platforms, it's worth understanding why traders leave Polymarket in the first place:

- **US access restrictions** — Polymarket blocks US IP addresses due to regulatory concerns

- **Market gaps** — some events you want to trade simply aren't listed
- **Liquidity concerns** — smaller markets can have wide spreads

Each of these problems points to a different alternative. The right choice depends on why you're leaving.

## 2. Kalshi — Best for US Users

Kalshi is the only CFTC-regulated prediction market exchange in the United States. That regulatory approval is significant: it means Kalshi can legally offer prediction market contracts to US users as financial instruments.

**Key facts:**
- Regulated by the CFTC as a Designated Contract Market
- Accepts USD deposits (no crypto required)
- Focuses on economic events, politics, weather, and sports
- $850 per-contract position limit on political markets (regulatory requirement)

**What it does better than Polymarket:**
- US legal, fiat deposits, regulatory protection
- Cleaner interface for traditional finance users

**What it does worse:**
- Smaller market selection than Polymarket
- Position limits constrain larger traders
- Less crypto-native market coverage

**Best for:** US-based traders who want a regulated, fiat-friendly platform.

## 3. Manifold Markets — Best for Beginners

Manifold is a play-money prediction market where you never risk real funds. Anyone can create a market on any topic, and you trade with "mana"—the platform's free virtual currency.

**Key facts:**
- No real money involved (free to join and trade)
- Community-created markets on any topic
- Excellent for learning prediction market mechanics
- Active community of thousands of forecasters

**What it does better than Polymarket:**
- Zero financial risk—perfect for learning
- More diverse market topics (niche events not found elsewhere)
- Stronger forecasting community and track record data

**What it does worse:**
- No real money = no real stakes = less accurate pricing on major events
- Less liquidity on any given market
- Not useful if you want to profit from your predictions

**Best for:** New users learning how prediction markets work, or anyone who wants to track forecasts without financial exposure.

## 4. Metaculus — Best for Serious Forecasters

Metaculus is a forecasting platform focused on long-range, high-stakes predictions—think geopolitical events, scientific breakthroughs, and macro economic trends. It doesn't involve money; instead, it uses a points system that tracks forecaster accuracy over time.

**Key facts:**
- No financial bets—pure forecasting reputation
- Long time horizons (months to years)
- Used by researchers, think tanks, and policy analysts
- Rigorous resolution criteria

**Best for:** Analysts who want to track long-term prediction accuracy, or researchers studying forecasting methodology.

## 5. PredictIt — US Politics Specialist

PredictIt is one of the oldest US-based prediction market platforms, operating under a CFTC no-action letter specifically for political markets. It's strictly limited to US political events.

**Key facts:**
- Focus: US elections, legislation, political appointments
- $850 position limit per contract (regulatory requirement)
- Real money, USD deposits
- Declining market share as Kalshi and Polymarket have grown

**Best for:** US politics specialists who want a regulated, real-money option.

**Note:** PredictIt's no-action letter has faced uncertainty; check current status before depositing.

## 6. Augur — Fully Decentralized Option

Augur is a fully decentralized prediction market protocol built on Ethereum. It has no central authority—markets are created and resolved by the protocol itself.

**Key facts:**

- Requires ETH/DAI to trade
- No KYC required
- Very low liquidity compared to Polymarket or Kalshi

**Best for:** Users who prioritize decentralization and censorship resistance above all else, and are comfortable with lower liquidity.
